Investment Options
A number of companies have taken some of the hassle of choosing and managing investments by doing it all for you.

Why use one of these companies?
It takes about 20 minutes to sign up: you choose to invest an amount each month, the risk level you are comfortable with and an optional principle amount then they take care of the rest. This means all the painstaking tasks like picking what and when to buy and sell, reinvesting dividends, re-balancing etc etc.

Why do these companies exist?
These companies are, of course, trying to make money too. So, for the trouble of managing your investments, they take a small percentage, usually between 0.3% and 1% - which can really make the difference (more on this later). However, for hassle free investing you it's not a bad deal.

Now, of course the value of your portfolio (stocks and shares the companies buy with your investments) can go up and down but that's really where you beauty of investing over the long term comes in, because although history is no guarantee of the future it can be a good indicator. Whenever there has been a stock market crash, it has always recovered within a number of years.

Information
I would like to emphasise, all of what I'm saying is advice, and your portfolio (investments) can lose money as well as gain money. That said if you can train yourself to deal with short term volitility (ups and downs) then long term (10, 20, 30, 40 years) it's highly unlikely that you'll lose money, in fact it would be astounding if you didn't return a very good return on investment.

Key points
  1. Invest small amounts over time to keep the highs and lows less intense.
  2. Think about what you want to achieve - I'm advocating low term investments because of the power of compound interest but think about yourself and what you want.
  3. Stick to the plan - do not panic and sell / withdraw even in the event your portfolio takes a dip.
  4. Choose a risk level you are comfortable with to help avoid panic selling / withdrawing.
